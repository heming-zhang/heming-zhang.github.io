About

Now, I am PhD candidate in Washington University School of Medicine(WUSM). My research interests span Agentic AI, Large Reasoning Models (LRMs) and Precision Medicine.

In 2021, my scholarly journey culminated in the successful procurement of a Master of Science in Computer Science from Washington University. During my master's study, I joined Dr. Fuhai Li's Lab as a research assistant in Institute for Informatics, Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis to use computational and deep learning models, especially graph neural networks, to analyse cells signaling interactions and predict drug combinations effect.

And I finished my Bachelor degree in Information Systems in 2019 as "Boya" Plan Student and won the honor of "Outstanding Graduate" from Central China Normal University(CCNU), where I mainly carried out researches on text mining.

In 2018, I obtained the fellowship from Mitacs to become a VIRS in UBC with helpful supervision of Dr. Ivan Beschastnikh to carry on research about system machine learning.

Projects

Recent Major

Develop an AI Co-Scientist for Autonomous Scientific Reasoning in Biomedicine

April 2025 - Present

Funded by NLM R01 LM013902; NIA R21 AG078799; NIA R56 AG065352; NINDS RM1 NS132962

  • Design and improve OmniCellAgent—built on BioMedGraphica and Graph-Language Foundation Models (GLFMs)—integrating data querying, GLFM inference, and explanation generation in a closed loop to produce interpretable hypotheses (e.g., target prioritization, drug-combination suggestions).
  • Finetune the agent orchestrator to enhance task planning using reinforcement learning with reward modeling.
  • Establish a benchmark using expert knowledge, literature alignment, and human feedback to evaluate scientific reasoning in biomedical agentic AI.

Build Biomedical AI Ecosystem for Interpretable Multi-Omics Integration and Discovery

February 2024 - Present

Funded by NLM R01 LM013902; NIA R21 AG078799; NIA R56 AG065352; NINDS RM1 NS132962

  • Preprocess DNA methylation data based on CpG sites, then fuse multi-omics (epigenomic/genomic/transcriptomic/proteomic) into a signaling graph by mosGraphGen to generate a graph-AI ready dataset.
  • Build BioMedGraphica, an all-in-one platform that harmonizes biomedical nomenclature across fragmented resources and, from user-specified inputs, auto-generates a Text–Numeric Graph (TNG)—a novel data format bridging textual biological priors with quantitative features—to develop Graph–Language Foundation Models (GLFMs). To facilitate broad adoption, the platform is released as a public web interface.
  • Release two ontology-standardized, distribution-calibrated TNG datasets: (1) OmniCellTOSG for single-cell data (CellxGene, GEO, BrainCellAtlas) and (2) MOTASG for bulk omics data (TCGA, DepMap, Synapse), enabling GLFMs pretraining and benchmarking downstream tasks for disease classification, survival prediction, CRISPR essentiality, drug response, and cell-type annotation.
  • Enable interpretability via attention-based attribution or reinforcement-guided subgraph reasoning for mechanistic insights.
